Welcome to science class

The **solar system** is a vast and dynamic collection of celestial objects bound together by the gravitational pull of the **Sun**, which lies at its center. It formed about **4.6 billion years ago** from a giant cloud of gas and dust that collapsed under gravity, creating the Sun and a rotating disk of leftover material. From this disk, planets, moons, asteroids, and comets gradually formed. The solar system consists of: ### **1. The Sun** A massive star made mostly of hydrogen and helium, the Sun provides the heat and light necessary for life on Earth. Its gravity keeps all other objects in orbit. ### **2. The Planets** There are **eight major planets**, which fall into two groups: * **Inner rocky planets:** Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars * **Outer gas/ice giants:** Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune ### **3. Moons** Many planets have natural satellites. For example, Earth has one moon, while Jupiter and Saturn each have dozens. ### **4. Smaller Bodies** These include: * **Asteroids** (mostly in the Asteroid Belt between Mars and Jupiter) * **Comets** (icy bodies that develop glowing tails when near the Sun) * **Dwarf planets** like Pluto, Eris, and Ceres * **Meteoroids**, which can enter Earth’s atmosphere as meteors ### **5. The Outer Regions** Beyond Neptune lies the **Kuiper Belt**, a region filled with icy objects, and even farther is the **Oort Cloud**, a distant spherical shell believed to house trillions of icy bodies. Overall, the solar system is a complex and fascinating environment, helping scientists understand both our own planet and the universe beyond. Some fun facts

Here are some interesting and important **facts about water**: ### **Basic Scientific Facts** * Water is made of **two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom

WATER BOILS AT 100o * It is the **only substance on Earth** that naturally exists in all three states: solid (ice), liquid, and gas (water vapor). * Water covers about **71% of Earth’s surface**. * About **97% of Earth’s water is saltwater**, and only **3% is freshwater**—most of which is locked in glaciers and ice caps. ### **Unique Properties** * Water is known as the **universal solvent** because it can dissolve more substances than any other liquid. * It has a **high specific heat capacity**, meaning it can absorb a lot of heat without changing temperature quickly—this helps regulate Earth’s climate. * Ice is **less dense than liquid water**, which is why it floats. * Water molecules are strongly attracted to each other (cohesion) and to other surfaces (adhesion). ### **Biological Importance** * The human body is made of about **60% water**. * Water is crucial for digestion, circulation, temperature control, and eliminating waste. * All known forms of life depend on water. ### **Environmental and Global Facts** * Around **1 in 3 people worldwide** lack access to safe drinking water. * Agriculture uses about **70% of the world’s freshwater supply**. * Oceans produce **over half of the world's oxygen**, thanks to marine plants and phytoplankton. ### **Fun Facts** * Hot water can freeze faster than cold water under certain conditions—this is called the **Mpemba effect**. * A single tree can release **hundreds of liters of water** into the air every day through transpiration. * Most of the freshwater on Earth is **frozen as ice**—mainly in Antarctica.
